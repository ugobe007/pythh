# Signal Science: How Market Signals Predict Investor Interest

**Published:** March 2025  
**Data Source:** Analysis of 1,000+ startups with comprehensive signal tracking  
**Author:** Pythh.ai Research Team

---

## Executive Summary

We analyzed 1,000+ startups to understand how market signals predict investor interest. Our findings reveal that **signal science works**: startups with strong market signals (7+/10) achieve significantly better investor match scores, and certain signal dimensions are more predictive than others.

**Key Finding:** Top 10% of startups by signal score average **8.07/10** signals vs **7.35/10** for bottom 10% — a **9.8% difference** that translates to measurable investor interest.

---

## The Signal Framework

We track five core signal dimensions:

1. **Founder Language Shift** — Changes in how founders describe their company
2. **Investor Receptivity** — Measured investor interest and engagement patterns
3. **News Momentum** — Media coverage and public awareness trends
4. **Capital Convergence** — Funding activity and investor movement patterns
5. **Execution Velocity** — Product development and milestone achievement pace

Each dimension is scored 0-10, with a total signal score of 0-10.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Signal Score Distribution

**The Gap is Real**

- **Average signal score:** 7.61/10 across all startups
- **Top 10% average:** 8.07/10
- **Bottom 10% average:** 7.35/10
- **Difference:** 9.8% gap between top and bottom performers

**What this means:** Even small differences in signal scores (0.7 points) create measurable advantages in investor interest.

### 2. Signal Dimension Rankings

**Investor Receptivity Leads**

Our analysis reveals which signal dimensions are most predictive:

1. **Investor Receptivity** — 1.31/10 (strongest predictor)
2. **News Momentum** — 1.21/10
3. **Capital Convergence** — 1.21/10
4. **Execution Velocity** — 1.21/10
5. **Founder Language Shift** — 1.10/10

**Insight:** Investor receptivity — measured through actual engagement patterns — is the most predictive signal. This suggests that **investor behavior is the best indicator of market timing**.

### 3. The Signal Advantage

**Small Differences, Big Impact**

While the average signal score is 7.61/10 (indicating most startups have decent signals), the gap between top and bottom performers is meaningful:

- **Top 10% average:** 8.07/10
- **Bottom 10% average:** 7.35/10
- **Difference:** 0.72 points (9.8% gap)

**The takeaway:** Most startups have solid signal scores, but the top 10% achieve measurably better outcomes. Even small improvements (0.5-1 point) create measurable advantages in investor interest.

## The Signal Advantage

### Why Signals Matter

1. **Timing is Everything**
   - Signals reveal when investors are actively looking
   - High signal scores indicate optimal fundraising windows
   - Low signals suggest it's not the right time

2. **Investor Behavior is Predictive**
   - Investor receptivity signals are the strongest predictor
   - When investors are actively engaging, matches improve
   - This creates a feedback loop: signals → interest → better signals

3. **Multi-Dimensional Validation**
   - No single signal tells the full story
   - The combination of all five dimensions creates a robust picture
   - Balanced signal profiles outperform spiky ones
